01 / PRIVACY Recommended
AV

AliasVault

Passwords, aliases, and private identities in one encrypted vault.

Best for: privacy-conscious people who want a password manager to protect more than passwords. AliasVault can create a different identity, email address, and login for every service you use.

Why it stands out

  • Client-side, end-to-end encrypted vault with a zero-knowledge design.
  • Built-in email aliases and virtual identities instead of a separate add-on.
  • Stores passwords, passkeys, and 2FA codes with browser and mobile access.
  • Open source, with both an official hosted service and Docker self-hosting.
Keep in mind

It is a younger ecosystem than the largest password managers. Check that its sharing and migration options fit your needs, and only self-host if you want responsibility for updates and email delivery.

02 / MEDIA Recommended
YT

Yamtrack

One self-hosted home for nearly everything you watch, read, and play.

Best for: self-hosters who are tired of keeping separate lists for movies, TV, anime, manga, games, books, comics, and board games.

Why it stands out

  • Detailed progress, scores, notes, rewatches, history, seasons, and episodes.
  • Custom entries, personal or collaborative lists, release calendars, and notifications.
  • Imports from Trakt, Simkl, MyAnimeList, AniList, and Kitsu.
  • Connects with Jellyfin, Plex, and Emby to record watched media automatically.
Keep in mind

Yamtrack is a self-hosted web app, so you provide the server and maintenance. Docker makes setup approachable, but a zero-maintenance hosted tracker will be simpler if you only need a basic watchlist.
